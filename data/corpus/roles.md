# Team roles

A CS2 team is five players with distinct jobs. Roles overlap and shift round to
round, but most teams assign these responsibilities.

## Entry fragger
The first player into a contested space on a T execute. Their job is to win or
trade the opening duel and create space for the rest, even at the cost of dying
first. Entry fraggers need confident aim and a willingness to take the hardest
fight on the round.

## Support
Plays alongside the entry, throwing the flashes and smokes that let the entry
win their duel, and trading kills immediately. Support players sacrifice
personal stats for utility and trades.

## Lurker
Separates from the team to gather information, cut off rotations, and catch
defenders out of position. A good lurk applies pressure on the far side of the
map so the main execute meets fewer defenders, and threatens flanks late in the
round.

## AWPer
The dedicated sniper, holding key long angles on defense and taking early picks
on offense. Because the AWP costs $4750, the AWPer's buy is planned around the
team economy and they are expected to convert picks into round wins.

## In-game leader (IGL)
The shot-caller who decides buys, calls strategies and mid-round adaptations,
and manages the team's tempo and economy. The IGL often plays a lower-mechanical
role so they can focus on reading the game, and is the person who decides when
to force, save, or commit to a site.
