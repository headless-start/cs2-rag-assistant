# Vertigo callouts and overview

Vertigo is set on a high-rise construction site, and its gimmick is the
environment itself: there are open edges with no railings, and falling off the
building is instant death, so positioning near ledges is genuinely dangerous.

## A site
A is reached through **A ramp** and **A main**, onto a site with **CT**,
**generator**, and the default plant among the construction scaffolding. CTs
hold from CT and ramp; the close angles mean utility and a careful entry matter
more than long-range aim.

## B site
B is approached through **B stairs** and the **mid** route, with **CT** and the
back of the site as holding spots. The enclosed corridors make B a close,
utility-driven fight.

## Mid
**Mid** connects the sides and includes the infamous open edges; pushing mid
risks both enemy fire and the drop. Controlling mid and the ramps is the usual
key to opening a site.

## How rounds tend to play
The tight corridors and lethal ledges make Vertigo a close-quarters, utility-
heavy map where map knowledge and not walking off the edge matter as much as
aim. Ts commit to one site through the ramps; CTs hold close angles and rotate
through mid.
