# Competitive format and ranks

## MR12
CS2 standardized competitive play on the **MR12** format: a maximum of 12 rounds
per half, with teams switching sides (T and CT) at halftime. The first team to
**13** round wins takes the map. This replaced the older MR15 / first-to-16
format and makes matches shorter and economy swings sharper, since there are
fewer rounds to recover a broken economy.

## Halftime and overtime
Teams swap sides after 12 rounds. If the score reaches **12-12**, the map goes
to **overtime**, played in short MR-based blocks with a fresh starting budget
each overtime half, repeating until one team pulls ahead by the required margin.

## Premier mode and the rating
**Premier** is CS2's flagship ranked mode. It uses a **CS Rating**, a numeric
skill number shown on a leaderboard, rather than the old badge-style ranks.
Premier also uses a **map pick/ban** phase before the match, mirroring how
professional matches choose maps. Casual **Competitive** matchmaking still uses
the older per-map skill-group badges.

## Why the format matters
Because MR12 gives each team only 12 rounds a half, every economy decision
carries more weight: there is less time to climb out of a losing streak, so
forces, saves, and anti-ecos are higher-stakes than they were under the longer
format.
