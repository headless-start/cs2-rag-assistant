# Economy: buying as a team

Buying is a team decision, not an individual one. The goal is to have five
players fully equipped on the same round, because four rifles and a pistol lose
to five rifles. Reading your own and the enemy's money is a core skill.

## Round types
- **Full buy** — rifle, armor with helmet, and a full set of grenades for
  everyone. This is the round you want to win and the one you plan around.
- **Eco (save)** — buy little or nothing, keep pistols, and save money so the
  team can full-buy next round. Players often still buy a P250 or armor.
- **Force buy** — when the team cannot afford a full buy but cannot afford to
  give up the round either (for example, match point against you), everyone
  spends everything on SMGs, shotguns, armor, and cheap rifles.
- **Half buy / anti-eco** — a lighter buy (SMGs, armor, some utility) used to
  punish an enemy you expect to be on a weak buy, since SMG and shotgun kills
  pay extra.

## When to save vs force
The default rule of thumb: if a full buy this round would leave you unable to
full buy next round after a likely loss, save instead and come back strong. A
broken half-buy that loses tends to keep a team poor for several rounds. Forcing
is justified when losing the round is as bad as losing your economy — late in a
half, on match point, or when the enemy is also weak.

## Saving weapons
On a lost round, surviving players should retreat and keep their guns rather
than trade them for one more frag. A saved rifle is one less rifle the team has
to rebuy, which can turn a forced eco into a near-full buy.

## Dropping
A player with money should buy weapons or armor for teammates who are short.
Coordinated drops let a team that is split on money still field a unified buy.
