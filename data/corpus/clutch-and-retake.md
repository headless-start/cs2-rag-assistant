# Clutches and retakes

## Clutching (1vX)
A clutch is winning a round as the last player alive against one or more
opponents. The core ideas:
- **Isolate duels.** Use angles, sound, and position so you only fight one enemy
  at a time instead of being caught in a crossfire.
- **Play the clock and the objective.** If the bomb is down and you are CT, you
  must retake and defuse; if you are T post-plant, you can play for time and let
  the timer win the round for you.
- **Use information and silence.** Stop moving to listen, fake a defuse to bait a
  push, and deny the enemy your footsteps.
- **Bait the defuse.** A T can let a CT start defusing, then catch them locked in
  the animation — the same trick works in reverse with a fake defuse.

## Retaking a site
A retake is the CTs taking back a site after the bomb is planted. It is hard
because the Ts hold known post-plant angles and the clock is against the CTs.
Keys to a good retake:
- **Commit together with utility.** Flashes and HE grenades clear post-plant
  positions and a smoke can split the defenders' vision before CTs swing in.
- **Trade, don't solo.** Entering one at a time feeds the post-plant; entering
  together forces multiple fights at once.
- **Account for the kit.** With a defuse kit the defuse is five seconds, which
  changes whether you have time to clear an angle before defusing or must defuse
  under pressure and hold the enemy off.
