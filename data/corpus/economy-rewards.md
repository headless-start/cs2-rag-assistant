# Economy: money and rewards

CS2 is as much an economy game as a shooter. Managing money across a team is
often what separates good teams from good aimers. Each player starts the first
round of a half with $800, and the most anyone can hold is $16,000.

## Kill rewards
Money per kill depends on the weapon, not the target:
- Most rifles and pistols: **$300**
- SMGs: **$600** (the P90 is the exception at **$300**)
- Shotguns: **$900**
- AWP: **$100**; SSG 08 / Scout and auto-snipers: **$300**
- Knife: **$1500**

The low AWP reward is deliberate — it stops AWPers from snowballing money on top
of impact.

## Round-win rewards
- Winning by eliminating the enemy team or running out the clock: **$3250**.
- Terrorists winning by bomb detonation: **$3500**.
- The player who defuses the bomb earns an extra **$300**; the player who plants
  it earns an extra **$300**.

## Loss bonus
A team that loses a round earns a consolation payout that grows with each
*consecutive* loss: **$1400, $1900, $2400, $2900, $3400**, capped at $3400. The
ladder steps back down after a win. This is what lets a team that has been
losing slowly rebuild toward a full buy.

## The plant bonus
If the terrorists plant the bomb but still lose the round, the whole team
receives an **$800** bonus regardless of where they are on the loss ladder.
This is why planting — even on a lost round — is almost always worth it for the
T economy.
