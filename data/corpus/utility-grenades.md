# Grenades and utility

Utility wins rounds at a high level more than aim does. A team can take or hold
a site without firing a shot if the grenades are right. Each player can carry up
to four grenades, but only one of each of the HE, smoke, and flash-type slots,
so loadouts are a trade-off.

## Smoke grenade ($300)
Creates a large cloud that blocks vision for both sides. Used to cut off
angles, block a choke, screen a bomb plant or defuse, and split a site. In CS2
smokes are volumetric and react to the world (see the smoke mechanics note).

## Flashbang ($200)
Detonates with a blinding flash. An enemy looking toward it is blinded for
longer; looking away or turning at the right moment reduces the effect.
Pop-flashes thrown so the enemy has no time to react are a core entry tool. You
can carry two flashes if you skip another grenade.

## HE grenade ($300)
Deals area damage, strong for softening grouped enemies, finishing a low-health
opponent behind cover, or clearing a common hiding spot. It also briefly opens a
hole in a smoke.

## Molotov ($400, T) and Incendiary ($600, CT)
Both spread fire over an area that deals damage over time and denies the ground
entirely while it burns. Used to block a choke, flush an enemy off a position,
delay a push, or stop a defuse. The incendiary burns out slightly faster than
the molotov.

## Decoy ($50)
A cheap grenade that mimics gunfire sounds and shows fake radar blips, used to
fake activity on one side of the map while the team executes elsewhere.
