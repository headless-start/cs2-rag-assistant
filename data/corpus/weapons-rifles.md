# Rifles

Rifles are the backbone of a full buy. They are accurate when standing still or
counter-strafing and fall off quickly while running, so movement discipline
matters more than raw aim.

## AK-47 (Terrorist, $2700)
The AK is the defining T-side rifle. It kills with a single headshot through a
helmet at almost any range, which is why it is the most feared weapon in the
game. The trade-off is a sharp, hard-to-control recoil pattern: the first
shots climb, then the spray pulls to the right and back left. Most players tap
or burst at distance and only spray up close.

## M4A4 (Counter-Terrorist, $2900)
The standard CT rifle. It does not one-shot a helmeted head, so CT duels lean
on controlled bursts and good crosshair placement. It carries 30 rounds with a
large reserve and has a forgiving spray, making it the easier of the two M4s to
learn.

## M4A1-S (Counter-Terrorist, $2900)
The silenced alternative to the M4A4. It is more accurate with lower recoil and
hides the shooter's position and tracers, but it holds only 20 rounds in the
magazine with a small reserve, so ammo management is real. Players pick it for
holding angles and long-range control.

## AUG and SG 553 (scoped rifles)
The AUG ($3300, CT) and SG 553 ($3000, T) add a zoom scope for accurate
mid-to-long range fire, and the SG one-shots a helmeted head like the AK. They
reward holding angles but are heavier and slower to bring up than the standard
rifles.

## Galil AR and FAMAS (budget rifles)
The Galil ($1800, T) and FAMAS ($1950, CT) are cheaper rifles used on
force-buys and anti-eco rounds. They are less accurate and do less damage than
the AK and M4 but let a team contest a round without a full buy.
