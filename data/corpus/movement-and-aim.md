# Movement and shooting mechanics

Counter-Strike's shooting model rewards stillness and punishes movement, so
mechanics are as much about your feet as your crosshair.

## Counter-strafing
Bullets are only accurate when the player is nearly stationary. Counter-strafing
is tapping the opposite movement key to cancel your momentum instantly, so you
go from running to a dead stop and your first shot lands true. It is the single
most important movement skill for winning duels.

## Spray patterns and recoil
Each weapon has a fixed recoil pattern: the bullets climb and then sweep in a
repeatable shape. Players learn to pull the mouse in the opposite direction to
"control the spray." At range, tapping or short bursts that let the gun reset
are more reliable than holding the trigger.

## Tagging
Getting shot slows you down ("tagging"), which makes it harder to escape a fight
once it starts and rewards whoever lands the first shots. This is why being
already stopped and aimed when contact happens is so valuable.

## Peeking techniques
- **Jiggle peek** — briefly swinging out and back to bait a shot or gather
  information without committing.
- **Shoulder peek** — exposing just enough to draw an AWP shot you can dodge,
  so a teammate can punish the reload.
- **Wide / swing peek** — taking an angle with momentum to catch a static
  defender, relying on counter-strafing to stop and fire.

## Crosshair placement
Keeping the crosshair at head level and pre-aimed at the spot an enemy will
appear means you only have to move horizontally to land the shot, which beats
reacting from the hip.
