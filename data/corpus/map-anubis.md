# Anubis callouts and overview

Anubis is an Egyptian-themed map that entered the active competitive pool in the
CS2 era. Its defining feature is the water canals running through the map, which
open unusual paths and make mid and connector control central.

## Mid and water
**Mid** drops toward the **water** and **canal** running across the map, with a
**connector** linking mid into A. The water routes give Ts flanking options and
make CT rotations and crossfires important. Controlling mid and connector is the
usual key to opening a site.

## A site
Approached from **A main** and from mid through **connector**. The site has
**palace** overlooking it and open angles that reward a smoke on CT and a flash
over before entry. CTs hold from CT and palace.

## B site
Reached through **B main** and via the water/connector routes. It is a more
open site with multiple angles, so CTs spread their hold and Ts need smokes to
cut the crossfires before planting.

## How rounds tend to play
The water paths and open sites make Anubis a map of mid control and timing: Ts
use the canals to split defenders' attention, while CTs rely on crossfires and
fast rotations. Post-plant positions matter because the open sites are hard to
retake without utility.
