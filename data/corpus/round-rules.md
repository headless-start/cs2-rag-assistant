# Round rules and timers

## The objective
Counter-Strike is round-based. Terrorists (T) try to plant the C4 bomb at one
of two bombsites and have it detonate, or eliminate all CTs. Counter-Terrorists
(CT) try to prevent the plant, defuse a planted bomb, or eliminate all Ts. A
round can also end on the timer if the bomb is never planted.

## Timers
- **Freeze time** at the start of a round, when players buy but cannot move,
  is around 15-20 seconds depending on the mode.
- **Round time** is **1:55** (115 seconds) to plant the bomb. If it is never
  planted, CTs win when the clock runs out.
- The **bomb timer** is **40 seconds** from the plant to detonation. Once
  planted, the round no longer ends on the round clock — only on detonation,
  defuse, or elimination.

## Planting and defusing
- Planting the bomb takes about **3.2 seconds** of standing on a bombsite.
- Defusing takes **10 seconds** with bare hands, or **5 seconds** with a
  **defuse kit**. The defuse can be interrupted and must be restarted if the CT
  is forced off it.
- After a plant, the 40-second window forces CTs to either retake the site or
  lose, which is why post-plant positioning and utility are decisive.

## Winning a round
A round is won by elimination, by the bomb detonating (T) or being defused (CT),
or by the round timer expiring with no plant (CT). Each win moves the team one
step toward the score needed to win the match.
