# Pistols

Pistols decide the opening pistol round of each half and carry eco and
force-buy rounds. They are most accurate on the first shot, so tapping beats
spamming.

## Starting pistols
The Glock-18 ($200, T) fires fast with low recoil but low damage; its burst
mode trades accuracy for a quick three-round burst up close. The USP-S ($200,
CT) is silenced, accurate, and hits hard per shot but holds only 12 rounds. The
P2000 ($200) is the unsilenced CT alternative with a larger magazine.

## P250 ($300)
A cheap upgrade that deals strong armor-penetrating damage at short range and
one-shots an unarmored head. It is the staple eco-round pistol on both sides.

## Desert Eagle ($700)
High damage and a one-shot headshot at most ranges, but punishing recoil and a
slow rate of fire. It rewards precise tapping and is a common pick on the
pistol round and on ecos hunting for a big frag.

## Tec-9, Five-SeveN, and CZ75-Auto
The Tec-9 ($500, T) and Five-SeveN ($500, CT) are run-and-gun pistols with
large magazines and good armor penetration, strong on force-buys. The CZ75-Auto
($500) is a fully automatic pistol with a small magazine and a long reload,
deadly up close as a surprise weapon.

## Dual Berettas ($300) and R8 Revolver ($600)
The Dual Berettas spray two pistols at once for cheap close-range pressure. The
R8 Revolver hits like a Deagle but has a noticeable delay before the primary
shot fires, so it is a niche pick.
