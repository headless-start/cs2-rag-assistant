# Overpass callouts and overview

Overpass is set around a German canal and park, with the two bombsites at very
different elevations: **B site** is an enclosed underground room, while **A
site** sits up top in the open by the fountain and bathrooms.

## A side
A is approached through **long A** and **A short**, passing the **bank** and
**playground** areas, onto an open site with **bathrooms**, **fountain**, and
the **truck**. The site is exposed, so Ts smoke off the CT angles and the
connector before planting, and CTs hold from bathrooms and the back.

## B site
B is reached through **monster** and the **water** route into the enclosed
underground room, with **short B** and **heaven** as CT holding spots above and
beside it. Because it is a closed box, B fights are close-range and utility
clears the corners.

## Mid and the connector
**Mid** and the **connector** link the two sides and let CTs rotate or Ts take
map control to threaten both sites. Control of the long and short approaches to
A and of the water/monster path to B is what each round tends to revolve around.

## How rounds tend to play
The elevation split gives each site a distinct feel: open and utility-screened
on A, tight and close on B. Ts commit utility to take one approach; CTs delay
with molotovs and crossfires and rotate through mid.
