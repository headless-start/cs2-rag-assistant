# Round strategies

## Default
A default is a patient round where the T side spreads out, takes safe map
control, and gathers information without committing, waiting for a pick or a
read before deciding which site to hit. It keeps options open and forces the CTs
to respect every part of the map.

## Execute
A coordinated hit on one site using a scripted set of grenades — smokes to block
the CT angles, flashes to blind defenders, molotov to clear close corners —
thrown in sequence so the entry can take the site with minimal exposure.
Executes are how Ts beat a stacked site.

## Split
Attacking a single site from two directions at once so the defenders cannot
cover both. A split usually needs map control (often mid) first, then both
groups hit on a timing call together.

## Rush / fast push
Five players commit to one site immediately with little setup, trading speed for
utility. Rushes punish CTs who are out of position or rotating, and are common
on force-buys where the team lacks utility anyway.

## Mid control and fakes
Taking mid gives access to both sites and the ability to fake: show presence and
throw utility at one site, then rotate quickly to hit the other while the CTs
commit defenders the wrong way.

## Post-plant and retake
After planting, Ts play time — holding angles that cover the bomb so the 40-
second timer runs out. CTs retake by clearing the site together with utility
before they can defuse, using the kit to save the crucial five seconds.
