# Nuke callouts and overview

Nuke is unique because its two bombsites are stacked vertically inside a single
building — **A site** on the upper level and **B site** directly below it. This
makes rotations extremely fast for CTs and makes Nuke one of the most
CT-favored maps, so T sides need disciplined fakes and fast hits.

## Outside and the approaches
**Outside** wraps around the building from T toward the back. **Ramp** leads up
toward A from outside, **heaven** is the raised CT platform over A, and
**hell / lobby** sit below. **Squeaky** door and **main** hall are the common
entrances into the upper site.

## A site (upper)
Inside the upper hall the bomb is planted around **default**, with CTs holding
from **heaven**, **rafters**, and **CT red**. **Hut** and **mini** are nearby
holding spots. The fast rotate from B comes up through **vents** and the ramp.

## B site (lower)
The lower site is reached through **vents** (the crawl-through connecting the
levels), **secret**, and **ramp**. It is tighter and darker than A, and the
quick vent drop is how CTs reinforce or retake.

## How rounds tend to play
The vertical stack means a CT who hears a hit on one site can be on the other in
seconds, so T executes live or die on speed and on faking the rotate. Outside
control and vent control are the recurring battlegrounds.
